# Lee Yong Wook (이용욱)

**Professor of Korean Language and Literature, Jeonju University**  
Humanities Engineering · Artificial Nature Ontology · Media Aesthetics

---

I am a literary theorist and STS scholar working at the intersection of humanistic inquiry and emerging technology. My research asks: *under what conditions does culture become generatable — not copied, not edited, but generated?*

This question defines my current period of work, which I call the **Age of Technological Generation (기술생성시대)** — the epoch in which Artificial Nature and AGI become the technical substrate of cultural production itself.

My 30-year trajectory moves through four periods:

```
1995–2003   Deconstruction · Virtual Space Theory
2004–2012   Digital Narratology · Game Storytelling  
2013–2020   Humanities Engineering Theory
2021–now    Artificial Nature Theory · AI/Posthuman
```

## Theoretical Framework

The **Artificial Nature Theory (인공자연론)** holds that contemporary platforms constitute a second nature — structurally, not metaphorically. Eight foundational elements:

**Ontological layer** `물질` `데이터` `에너지` `연결망`  
**Platform layer** `정보` `알고리즘` `UI` `UX`

Key concepts in active development:
- 공진주체 (Co-resonance Subject)
- 기계세 (Machinocene)  
- 동기화율 (Synchronization Rate)
- 기술생성예술 (Technological Generation Art)
